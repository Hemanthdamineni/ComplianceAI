WHAT IS KYC?
KYC stands for Know Your Customer. It represents the mandatory foundational process by which our institution identifies and verifies the true identity of a client before establishing any financial relationship. This is achieved exclusively by obtaining and verifying Officially Valid Documents (OVDs) such as passports or national identity cards. The core objective of KYC is to ensure that financial infrastructure is not abused by criminals attempting to conceal illicit funds or finance illegal operations.

DIRECT_ANSWER:
KYC (Know Your Customer) is the process by which financial institutions verify the identity of customers using officially valid documents to prevent fraud, money laundering, and terrorist financing.

WHAT IS AML?
AML stands for Anti-Money Laundering. It refers to the overarching web of laws, regulations, and internal institutional procedures specifically designed to stop criminals from disguising illegally obtained funds as legitimate income. While KYC specifically validates who the customer is, AML focuses actively on what the customer is doing with their money, ensuring we systematically track, identify, and block the integration of "dirty" money into the financial system.

DIRECT_ANSWER:
AML (Anti-Money Laundering) refers to the collective laws, regulations, and internal operational procedures implemented by financial institutions to detect, prevent, and report the conversion of illegally acquired funds into legitimate assets.

WHAT IS CDD?
CDD stands for Customer Due Diligence. It is the investigative process where our employees gather actionable facts about a customer to accurately assess their risk profile. CDD goes beyond merely collecting ID cards (KYC); it involves understanding the customer's business intent, mapping out expected transaction volumes, and uncovering the ultimate beneficial owners of corporate formations to ensure the client’s stated wealth actually matches their financial reality.

DIRECT_ANSWER:
CDD (Customer Due Diligence) is the investigative process of deeply analyzing a customer's background, intended business nature, and source of wealth to accurately assess and assign their financial risk profile during and after onboarding.

WHAT IS STR?
STR stands for Suspicious Transaction Report. It is a highly confidential formal report submitted by the Principal Officer to the Financial Intelligence Unit (FIU-IND) regarding any transaction—regardless of its monetary value—that generates a reasonable assumption of illicit activity. STRs are filed when a transaction lacks an economic rationale, involves inexplicably complex structures, or is suspected to be tied to money laundering or terrorist financing. 

DIRECT_ANSWER:
STR (Suspicious Transaction Report) is a confidential alert explicitly filed with federal authorities detailing a financial transaction that lacks a legitimate economic purpose, involves forged data, or raises reasonable suspicions of criminal financing regardless of cash value.

[TOPIC: Definition of CTR (Cash Transaction Report)]

WHAT IS CTR?
CTR stands for Cash Transaction Report. Unlike the subjective STR, the CTR is an objective monthly report automatically generated and transmitted to the Financial Intelligence Unit (FIU-IND). It mathematically documents all cash transactions occurring at our institution that exceed a predetermined statutory threshold (e.g., massive cash drops exceeding Rupees 10 Lakhs or an integrally connected sequence of cash deposits breaching that limit collectively).

DIRECT_ANSWER:
CTR (Cash Transaction Report) is a mandatory, automated mathematical report filed to regulatory authorities that aggregates and tracks all physical cash transactions exceeding predefined statutory limits, completely irrespective of underlying suspicion.
